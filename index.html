About Me

I am currently a student at the University of Central Florida studying Mechanical Engineering and Bioengineering. I have a love for all things science, especially space and interdisciplinary topics. More than anything else, I want to help others. I always try to stay involved in community service, and am happiest when my work is directly helping people. My specific academic interests include nanomaterials, medical technology innovation, thermodynamics of systems, propulsion systems, application of materials sciences in mechanical systems, and prosthetic design/manufacturing. Those aside, my personal interests often revolve around striving to bake the perfect cinnamon rolls, making my friends laugh, running, playing piano, painting, and the quest for the world's best french fries.

Projects

Limbitless Arc Reactor

As an Engineering Intern for Limbitless Solutions, I designed and printed my own Arc Reactor replica. I programmed an Adafruit Circuit Playground in modified C to perform certain actions upon conditions. I also was taught how to airbrush and paint with the leading technology in the fine art field.

UCF Rocketry

I currently serve on a team of students at UCF to build a hybrid motor rocket that will be entered in an international competition. My involvement began in Summer 2019 with contributions to the operations and media subteam, and I now work on the electronics, payload, airframe, propulsion, and finance subteams . The competition we attend each year is the Spaceport America Cup in New Mexico

Limbitless Educational Documentary

During my internship at Limbitless Solutions, I partnered with the heads of film and branding to make the first episode of an educational video series. I covered the topic of 3D printing, and with the help from many in the lab, was the narrator and teacher in this mini documentary. To watch this video, click here

3D Printed Key Fob Holder

After finally being able to own a 3D printer of my own, my first project was a new key fob case to replace a broken one. This challenge was intended to test not only my printer's capabilities, but also to test my own capabilities for calibration and optimal design on a printer without support material.

Nanomaterial Fabrication

One of my ongoing projects is the creation of a novel nanomaterial for the delivery of cancer therapeutics to osteosarcoma patients. Patent pending, check back later for more information once it is filed!

Bone Screw Design

A project that I have been assisting with is the development of a biodegradable bone screw. My role was mostly on the design side, as I was responsible for the engineering design for machining.
